# Marketing Site Standards For Axiom

- Brand tone: premium, restrained, technical, selective, calm, high-trust.
- Never use low-frame phrases like "website building," "professional website building," or copy that sounds like a freelancer explaining technical choices.
- Prefer buyer-language over builder-language.
- Do not feature defensive comparisons against Wix, Squarespace, WordPress, or similar tools.
- Do not fabricate testimonials, metrics, logos, case studies, or proof.
- Premium feel should come from hierarchy, spacing, typography, composition, and restraint, not just dark backgrounds or flashy motion.
- Copy should be concise, commercially sharp, and confidence-driven.
- Navigation, CTAs, pricing/investment framing, and proof sections should always reinforce premium positioning.
- When editing public-facing pages, audit for redundancy, generic agency wording, and template-like design patterns.
